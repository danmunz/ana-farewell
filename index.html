Who is Ana Duque-Higgins, Spain's Next Top Diplomat?

By
Dan Munz, Ace Reporter
-
May 17, 2018

Later this summer, as warm winds blow across the Spanish countryside and summer turns to autumn, the people of Spain will welcome another momentous change: The arrival of Ana Duque-Higgins as Embassy Madrid's Cultural Affairs Officer. But who is this diplomat charged with strengthening America's cultural ties to one of our oldest allies? In case you missed the functionally-identical piece that aired five days ago on WAMU, the 'ica team is here to bring you the exclusive details.

But first, we would like to subtly remind you that Chinese influence in Africa is bad.

News of Ana's assignment hit Spain in September 2017. In October 2017, Catalonia voted to secede from Spain, though experts assure us that these developments are unrelated. As CAO, Ana will be responsible for creating harmony and mutual understanding between different groups, a skillset for which she is renowned throughout IIP. Having mastered the art of communicating over, through and past many a mansplainer, Ana will craft cultural programs to take on both desinformación and machoexplicación. In mediating diplomatic disputes, she will draw on lessons learned from years of resolving petty conflicts between whiny, self-absorbed individuals, as well as her experiences as a mom.

CAPTION HERE

Ana is already planning innovative ideas such as a program to give Spanish youth authentic American experiences such as "Drive Liam To Soccer Practice" or "Make Ava Quit Texting So Much." She also plans to found the Strong, Reliable Spanish Leaders in Youth network, or SRSLY, and has been assured by EUR/PD that copious resources are available for this priority initiative. She will also engage in culinary diplomacy, with a campaign to rebrand small microwaveable bowls of oatmeal as "breakfast tapas".

Speaking of campaigns, China is waging a campaign for cultural influence in Africa, which is bad.

Beyond coming up with bright ideas to advance public diplomacy, Ana is hard at work preparing for this difficult hardship assignment. She has purchased high-SPF sunblock and several new hats, moves that are part of an initiative she calls the "Minimum Pressure Campaign." Ana grew up speaking Spanish at home, and has served in Argentina, Colombia, and Mexico, so naturally FSI required her to complete additional Spanish training. She's also working to make sure she fits in with the rest of the team at post. Her Ambassador at post is a fellow Tar Heel, but is named Duke, leaving her unsure of what to do with all her "DOOK SUCKS" t-shirts.

You know what else sucks? Chinese influence in Africa.
